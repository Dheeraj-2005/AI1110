\textbf{\large NCERT(12.13.1.12)}\\[3pt]
\textbf{Question:} Assume that each born child is equally likely to be a boy or a girl. If a family has two children, what is the conditional probability that both are girls given that
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(1)] the youngest is a girl
\item[(2)] at least one is a girl
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Solution:} Let $B$ be the event that a child is a boy, and $G$ be the event that a child is a girl. We are given that the probability of each of these events is $\pr{B} = \pr{G} = \frac{1}{2}$.

We want to find the conditional probability that both children are girls given two different conditions:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(1)] The youngest child is a girl.
\item[(2)] At least one of the children is a girl.
\end{enumerate}

Let's start with (1) first.

Let $Y$ be the event that the younger child is a girl. Then we want to find $\pr{GG | Y}$, the probability that both children are girls given that the younger one is a girl.

Using Bayes' theorem, we have:
\begin{align}
\pr{GG | Y} &= \frac{\pr{Y | GG} \pr{GG}}{\pr{Y}} \
&= \frac{1 \cdot \frac{1}{4}}{\frac{1}{2}} \
&= \frac{1}{2}
\end{align}

Therefore, the conditional probability that both children are girls given that the youngest is a girl is $\frac{1}{2}$.

Now let's consider (2).

Let $O$ be the event that at least one of the children is a girl. Then we want to find $\pr{GG | O}$, the probability that both children are girls given that at least one is a girl.

Using Bayes' theorem again, we have:
\begin{align}
\pr{GG | O} &= \frac{\pr{O | GG} \pr{GG}}{\pr{O}} \
&= \frac{1 \cdot \frac{1}{4}}{\frac{3}{4}} \
&= \frac{1}{3}
\end{align}
Therefore, the conditional probability that both children are girls given that at least one is a girl is $\frac{1}{3}$.
