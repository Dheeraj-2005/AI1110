\textbf{\large NCERT(12.13.6.18)}\\[3pt]
\textbf{Question:} If $P(A|B) > P(A)$, then which of the following is correct? 

\begin{tabular}{ll}
(A) $P(B|A) < P(B)$ & (B) $P(A \cap B) < P(A) \cdot P(B)$ \\\

(C) $P(B|A) > P(B)$ & (D) $P(B|A) = P(B)$
\end{tabular}
\textbf{Solution:} We can write the given condition as: 
\begin{align*}
\Pr(A|B) &= \frac{\Pr(A \cap B)}{\Pr(B)} > \Pr(A)
\end{align*}

Multiplying both sides by $\Pr(B)$, we get:

\begin{align*}
\Pr(A \cap B) &> \Pr(A) \cdot \Pr(B)
\end{align*}

Dividing both sides by $\Pr(A)$, we get:

\begin{align*}
\frac{\Pr(A \cap B)}{\Pr(A)} &> \Pr(B) \\
\implies\Pr(B|A) &= \frac{\Pr(A \cap B)}{\Pr(A)} > \Pr(B) \\
\implies \Pr(B|A) &> \Pr(B)
\end{align*}

This is equivalent to option (C), so (C) is correct.
